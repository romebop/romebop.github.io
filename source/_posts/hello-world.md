I had a lot of fun setting up a personal site for the first time and learning about how static site generators work. I decided to go with [Hexo](https://hexo.io/) which runs on top of [Node.js](https://nodejs.org/en/). It seems to be among the more popular ones with lots of community driven content and plugins to play with. Hosting was conveniently available through [GitHub Pages](https://pages.github.com/), and things came together quite simply, which was nice.

#### Moving Forward

I don't have much blogging experience aside from keeping a [Tumblr](https://romebop.tumblr.com), which more or less ended up being a collection of random media I've come across and found interesting. I hope this blog can be a collection of more processed ideas about my interests, whether it's about music, technology, culture, or something else.

A big motivation for me in wanting to create this space has been the idea of documenting my progress as I continue to grow as a person and programmer. Being able to reflect on my thoughts and express them coherently seems useful, and I like the idea of being able to look back to see how far I've come, and then look forward to a place I'd like to reach. Hopefully the content may come useful to others in the future as well.

**This will be fun.**

*Let's get it.*
